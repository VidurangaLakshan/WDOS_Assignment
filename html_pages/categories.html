Turtle Categories

There are many species of turtles and sea turtles in Sri Lanka. Sri Lanka is home to five out of the seven species of sea turtles found worldwide.

Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas)

The Green turtle (Chelonia mydas) is one of the largest species of sea turtles and is found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world. They are named for the green color of their skin, which is due to the presence of algae in their fat tissues.

Location in Sri Lanka

Chelonia mydas are nesting at the Kosgoda Coastline in Sri Lanka

Olive Ridley Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea)

The olive ridley turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is considered the most abundant sea turtle in the world, with an estimated 800,000 nesting females annually. Adults are relatively small, weighing on average around 45 kg.

Location in Sri Lanka

This species is known to nest in Karaduwa, Maggona, Bundala, MArichchikaddi, Kalpitiya, Udappuwa, Hambantota, Kundakuliya, Chilaw, Negambo, MAtara, Weligama. They are common in the Bay of Bengal, seen especially along parts of the Tamil Nadu coastline.

Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta)

The loggerhead sea turtle is the largest hard-shelled turtle in the world. The average adult is about 90 cm (35 in) long and weighs around 135 kg (298 lb). However, large specimens may reach 280 cm (110 in) and 450 kg (1000 lb).

Location in Sri Lanka

They are found in south & south-east shore of Sri Lanka including Kosgoda & Rakawa. But they are also seen very rarely in Sri Lanka.

Hawksbill Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata)

The hawksbill turtle’s tapered head ends in a sharp point resembling a bird’s beak, hence its name. A further distinctive feature is a pair of claws adorning each flipper. Male hawksbills have longer claws, thicker tails, and somewhat brighter coloring than females.

Location in Sri Lanka

Nesting sites of Sri Lanka includes Kataduwa, Bentota, Udappuwa, Kosgoda, Karaduwa, Talaimannar, Jaffna, Galkissa, Talawila, Palatupana, Amadhauwa. These turtles are found mainly in the tropical regions of the Atlantic, Pacific, Indian Oceans & Mediterranean Sea.
