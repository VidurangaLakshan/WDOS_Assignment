Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project located along the southern coast of Sri Lanka.

What Happens Here

The hatchery was established to protect and conserve sea turtles by collecting vulnerable eggs from the beach and providing them with a safe environment to hatch. The hatchlings are kept in tanks until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. In addition to this, the hatchery also provides a safe haven for injured or sick turtles, where they are treated and cared for until they are healthy enough to be released back into the ocean. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is open to visitors, offering educational tours where visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles and the conservation efforts being made to protect them.
