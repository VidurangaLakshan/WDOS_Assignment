Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project located along the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka.

What Happens Here

The hatchery was established to protect and conserve sea turtles, which are under threat from various factors such as habitat loss, poaching, and pollution. The hatchery collects eggs from vulnerable nests and provides a safe environment for them to hatch. Once the hatchlings are born, they are kept in tanks until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery also provides a safe haven for injured or sick turtles, where they are treated and cared for until they are healthy enough to be released back into the ocean. The hatchery is open to visitors and offers educational tours, where visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles and the conservation efforts being made to protect them.
